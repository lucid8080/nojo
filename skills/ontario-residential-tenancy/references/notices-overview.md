# LTB notice families (overview)

**Purpose:** Help choose **which official form and instruction sheet to open next**. This is not a substitute for reading the current form, its instructions, or the RTA.

**Rule:** Complete fields using the **official PDF** and its published instructions. Service and timing rules vary by form and situation.

## Commonly discussed notices (illustrative)

| Form (common reference) | Typical use (non-exhaustive) |
|-------------------------|------------------------------|
| **N4** | Notice relating to rent arrears (non-payment)—often a prerequisite step before certain rent-related applications, subject to rules |
| **N5** | Notice relating to substantial interference with reasonable enjoyment, damage, overcrowding—subject to statutory thresholds and cure periods where applicable |
| **N7** | Notice for very serious issues in defined circumstances—rare; strict requirements |
| **N8** | Notice relating to persistent late payment of rent—where statutory tests are met |
| **N12** | Notice for landlord’s own use, purchaser’s own use, or caregiver—specific requirements and compensation may apply |
| **N13** | Notice for demolition, conversion, repairs requiring vacant possession—specific requirements |

Exemptions (e.g. certain institutional, transitional, or non-RTA situations) may apply. **Confirm** the tenancy is covered by the RTA before selecting a form.

## What the agent should do

1. Identify the user’s goal (e.g. end tenancy for arrears vs conduct).
2. Point to the **matching official form** and instruction sheet.
3. List **facts still needed** (amounts, dates, names, service method).
4. **Never** fill in legal conclusions the user has not established (e.g. “substantial” interference).

## Disclaimer

Not legal advice. Form numbers, names, and rules change—verify against Ontario/LTB materials.
