# Communication patterns (email and letters)

## Principles

- **Factual:** Dates, amounts, and requested actions stated clearly.
- **Professional:** Neutral tone; no insults, threats, or discriminatory content.
- **Documented:** Prefer written channels when a record matters; note how and when sent.
- **Bounded:** Do not promise legal outcomes; do not claim a notice is “valid” without verification.

## Structure that usually works

1. **Subject line:** Unit address + short topic (e.g. “Rent account — [address]”).
2. **Opening:** Identify parties and unit.
3. **Facts:** Chronological, with references to lease or prior notices if applicable.
4. **Request or information:** What you need from the other party, or what you are providing.
5. **Timeline:** Clear date for response or action where appropriate.
6. **Closing:** Contact method; avoid ultimatums that sound like self-help eviction.

## Escalation and safety

- Genuine safety risks: prioritize emergency services and appropriate agencies; be careful not to minimize harassment or violence.
- Persistent conflict: suggest LTB or legal help rather than repeated informal demands.

## Records

Keep copies of outgoing and incoming messages, receipts, rent ledgers, and notices served. Users may need these for LTB or settlement discussions.

## Disclaimer

Not legal advice. Tone and content must fit the facts and any duty to accommodate or other legal obligations.
