# Ontario residential tenancy (support workflow)

## Jurisdiction and scope

- **In scope:** Ontario **residential** rental housing governed by the *Residential Tenancies Act, 2006*, S.O. 2006, c. 17 (RTA), as commonly applied before the Landlord and Tenant Board (LTB), plus practical communication and record-keeping for landlords, tenants, and their representatives in that context.
- **Out of scope unless the user clarifies:** Commercial tenancies; co-op or social housing with separate statutory regimes; other provinces; federal human rights complaints (except noting overlap); criminal matters; precise filing fees and deadlines that change—**verify on official sites** (see [references/official-sources.md](references/official-sources.md)).

## Safety and accuracy boundaries

- Outputs are **informational** and **not legal advice**. The user may need a lawyer, paralegal, or community legal clinic for their situation.
- **Do not** present drafts as guaranteed legally sufficient. **Do not** reproduce full text of official forms; point users to current PDFs from Ontario/LTB sources.
- Dates, amounts, service methods, and form versions **must** be confirmed against official materials. When unsure, say so and list what to verify.

## Default workflow

1. **Triage:** What does the user want (explain a rule, draft a letter, choose a notice type, prepare for an LTB step)?
2. **Confirm facts:** Parties, address/unit, tenancy start, rent and due date, what happened and when, prior notices or filings. See [references/intake-questions.md](references/intake-questions.md) for deeper checklists.
3. **Path:** Standard-form notice route vs informal communication vs legal referral. Use [references/notices-overview.md](references/notices-overview.md) for form families at a high level.
4. **Draft or explain:** With explicit assumptions and a **verification list** (dates, names, amounts, service).
5. **Next steps:** Records to keep, where to obtain official forms, and when to seek professional help.

## Intake: highest-signal questions

Before drafting any notice or formal letter, aim to know:

- **Parties:** Legal names; landlord (owner or authorized agent?) and tenants on the lease.
- **Premises:** Full rental address and unit identifier.
- **Tenancy:** Start date; periodic vs fixed; rent amount; due date; rent deposit/last month’s rent if claimed.
- **Issue:** Arrears (which months and amounts?), conduct, maintenance, unauthorized occupancy, end of tenancy, etc.
- **History:** Prior notices given; payments or agreements; any LTB file numbers or dates.

For expanded scenarios, use [references/intake-questions.md](references/intake-questions.md).

| Substantial interference, damage, overcrowding | **N5** family—see overview |
| Persistent late payment (where applicable) | **N8** family—see overview |
| Landlord’s own use / purchaser / demolition/repair | **N12** / **N13** families—see overview |
| End of term / no cause where permitted | Depends on facts and exemptions—**do not guess**; confirm RTA exceptions |
